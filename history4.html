Era of Good Feelings

National Unity & The Monroe Doctrine (1815–1825)

Key Chronology

1815 End of the War of 1812 leads to a surge in American nationalism.
1817 James Monroe is inaugurated; the "Era of Good Feelings" term is coined.
1819 The Panic of 1819 brings the first major financial crisis to the U.S.
1820 The Missouri Compromise is passed to settle slavery disputes in new states.
1823 President Monroe announces the "Monroe Doctrine" to the world.
1824 The "Corrupt Bargain" election ends the era of political unity.
