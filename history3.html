The Bill of Rights

The First Ten Amendments to the Constitution of the United States

Articles of Amendment

I. Freedom of Expression Religion, speech, press, assembly, and petition.
II. Right to Bear Arms The right of the people to keep and bear arms.
III. Quartering Soldiers No soldier shall be quartered in any house without consent.
IV. Search and Seizure Protection against unreasonable searches and seizures.
V. Rights of Persons Due process, double jeopardy, and self-incrimination.
VI. Rights in Criminal Cases Right to a speedy and public trial by an impartial jury.
VII. Rights in Civil Cases Right to a trial by jury in civil disputes.
VIII. Bails and Punishments No excessive bail, nor cruel and unusual punishments.
IX. Rights Retained by People Rights not listed are still held by the people.
X. Powers of the States Powers not delegated to the U.S. belong to the States.
